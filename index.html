# whoami

I'm a security analyst and M.Eng. Computer Science candidate at the University of Cincinnati (GPA 3.875) with hands-on SOC and Incident Response experience. I spent a year triaging live alerts in Splunk and Microsoft Sentinel, running phishing and ransomware investigations, and authoring the reports that postmortems and detection engineering were built on. I like the part of security where something has already gone wrong — reconstructing what an attacker did, in what order, and proving it with artifacts.

My work centers on business email compromise, ransomware triage, and Windows host forensics: pulling apart Event Logs, registry hives, Prefetch, Amcache/Shimcache and email headers, analyzing PCAPs for exfiltration, building defensible timelines, documenting IOCs, and mapping the kill chain to MITRE ATT&CK. I pair that with vulnerability management (Nessus / Qualys / OpenVAS) and an AWS & networking foundation, so I'm comfortable investigating across endpoints, email, and cloud.

# goal: join a SOC / IR / forensics team where I can triage real incidents, write the report that holds up, and harden what let the attacker in.
